Healthy Recipes for Working People

Nutritious, quick-to-prepare dishes are especially valuable to working people in the so-called gig economy and may segment their schedule into multiple "side hustles". You may fall under this category, or perhaps can relate: you have scarce time and may otherwise resort to other convenient options which may be less healthy to consume due to preservatives or fast food / fast casual restaraunt practices. Nonetheless, there are suitable meals which take only a few minutes to prepare, and you may even save your hard-earned cash!

Tuna Avocado Bowl

This is a nutrient packed dish which requires few ingredients. This dish is recommended for low carb and pescatarian diets Due to the relatively high mercury content of most store-bought canned tuna it isn't advisable to be consume this daily.

Estimated time: 3 - 5 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 Tuna Can (packed in olive oil advised) 1
  • 1 Avocado (sliced for ease)
  • 1/2 Red Onion
  • 1 tbsp Mayonnaise
  • 1 or 2 tsbp of Olive Oil (if tuna is packed in water)
  • Salt and Pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Slice and dice the onion. You may prefer a larger or smaller fraction.
  2. Empty tuna can into bowl and avocado slices. Mash together until avocado is reduced to fine chunks. Add a dollop of mayonnaise. 1 tbsp is sufficient although more may be added according to taste preference. Sprinkle onion bits.
  3. Add olive oil, and salt and pepper according to taste and dietary preferences.

Sausage and Veggies Pot

This is a highly portable dish which requires only as many ingredients as desired. Preparation time is variable. Assuming all ingredients are used one should set aside 20 minutes. The main advantage of this meal is it's portable and versatile. It's a good option for a family or a dinner date at home, and it can also be brought to the office. It also stays fresh over a week and it is therefore a good option for anyone who prefers to meal prep before the work week.

Estimated time: 15 - 20 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 cups baby potatoes, rinsed and quartered
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1/2 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1/2 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, halved and sliced
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 9 ounces smoked sausage, sliced (not ground sausage)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder or minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions

  1. In a heavy bottomed pot, heat 2 tablespoons olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the baby potatoes to the pot and season with salt and pepper. Fry until golden brown and cooked through, around 8-10 minutes, stirring a few times. Remove potatoes from the pot and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, brown the sliced sausage for around 5 minutes in 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium high heat. Remove the sausage from the pot and set aside.
  3. Add onion and garlic and cook for 1 to 2 minutes with remaining cooking oil. Add the bell peppers and zucchini to the same pot and season with red pepper flakes, Italian seasoning, salt and pepper. Cook for 2 minutes, until softened, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add the cooked sausage, and potatoes potatoes back to the pot and mix everything together. Add chicken broth and cook for 5 minutes more. Adjust seasoning and garnish with chopped fresh parsley. Ready to serve.
